Everybody talks about disruption, scalability, and so forth. When you read about this in your newspaper, you can be sure that the trend is so far ahead that you will never catch up. Hence it is imperative to deal with the technological questions of our time. However, if one listens to top executives, one often confronts a laissez-faire policy that postpones long-term strategies or outsources it to consultants. This procrastination strategy is disastrous. It leads to a stillstand an an ever-growing problem to make strategic decisions – a dilemma, that gets visible when it is too late. The banking-sector, still struggling to solve its IT-leaks, is a cautionary exampe. To confront the digital revolution, it is quintessential to internalize its principles.
